Global Warming & Climate Change

--------------------------------------------------
1. What is global warming?
Global warming = the Earth is getting hotter over time.

It means the average temperature of the Earth keeps rising.
This does not only change temperature.
It also changes weather, nature, and human life.

[Important] Global warming is about rising temperature.
[Important] Climate change is the bigger result of global warming.

--------------------------------------------------
2. What is climate change?
Climate change means long-term changes in weather patterns.

For example:
- hotter summers
- stronger storms
- more droughts
- heavy rain in some places
- melting ice
- rising sea levels

[Important] Climate change affects many parts of daily life.
[Important] It is not just about "hot weather."

--------------------------------------------------
3. Main causes
The biggest cause is human activity.

People burn fossil fuels like:
- coal
- oil
- natural gas

When these are burned, they release carbon dioxide into the air.
Too much carbon dioxide and other gases stay in the atmosphere and trap heat.

[Important] Burning fossil fuels increases greenhouse gases.
[Important] More greenhouse gases = more heat trapped on Earth.

--------------------------------------------------
4. Greenhouse effect
The greenhouse effect is a natural process.

Sunlight comes into the Earth.
The Earth absorbs heat.
Then some heat goes back out.

But greenhouse gases trap part of the heat.
This keeps the Earth warm.

This is normally a good thing,
because without it, Earth would be too cold for life.

The problem:
If there are too many greenhouse gases,
too much heat stays in the atmosphere.

[Important] The greenhouse effect is natural and necessary.
[Important] Too much greenhouse effect causes global warming.

--------------------------------------------------
5. Effects on nature
Climate change can seriously affect the environment.

Examples:
- glaciers melt
- sea level rises
- forests become drier
- animals lose their habitats
- some plants cannot survive

If habitats change too fast,
animals and plants may not be able to adapt.

[Important] Climate change can damage ecosystems.
[Important] Biodiversity may decrease if species cannot adapt.

--------------------------------------------------
6. Effects on people
Climate change also affects human life.

Examples:
- heat waves can make people sick
- floods can destroy homes
- droughts can reduce food production
- storms can damage cities and roads
- water supply may become unstable

Poorer areas are often affected more seriously.

[Important] Climate change is not only an environmental issue.
[Important] It is also a health, food, and safety issue.

--------------------------------------------------
7. What can we do?
We can take action in daily life.

Some simple ways:
- save electricity
- turn off lights when not needed
- use public transportation
- bring reusable cups or bags
- recycle properly
- waste less food

Governments and companies also need to help by using cleaner energy and making better policies.

[Important] Solving climate change needs teamwork.
[Important] Small actions matter if many people do them.

--------------------------------------------------
8. Final summary
Global warming is a serious problem in the modern world.
It changes the environment and also affects human society.

We should understand:
- what it is
- why it happens
- what problems it causes
- how we can help

[Important] Understanding the problem is the first step.
[Important] Protecting the Earth is everyone's responsibility.
